Backstory

It is the year of 2028. Humanity has finally broken new ground in settling Mars. It's a massive undertaking, sponsored and kick-started by numerous space agencies and space enterprises. Now new colonies are being established all over on this new continent, forming new human societies as we speak in this brave new world.

It is inspiring to see the development of diverse settlements taking shape. No two settlements look alike. One settlement starts from a humble four-person Lander Hab with and an Inflatable Greenhouse. Another settlement starst with a Lander Hab and a Fishery. Each settlement strives to meet its objectives. Each undertakes endeavors and embrace its destiny.

Without a doubt, there are also mining towns and trading outposts sprawling up and specializing in prospecting ores and minerals as they secure mining rights as quick as they can. Some find their fulfillment in manufacturing useful daily items and parts. Some prospect ores and mine precious metal and minerals. The resourcefulness of the pioneers — from those tending food crops to those maintaining our vehicle fleets and investigating the origin of life by surveying ancient dry lake beds — is what makes this brave new world possible.

Meanwhile, space tourists have also taken notice and are beginning to trickle in to get a taste of the ultimate adventure as well as visiting landmarks.

Mars's magnificent landscape is an untamed beauty and it can be hostile to the unsuspected. Has it always been a big, lifeless rock? Its past ecosystems, if any, await humanity's further investigations.

Reality is far from perfect, settlers still come face-to-face with meteorite impacts, radiation sickness, equipment malfunctions, accidents, illnesses, injuries, and even death. Sometimes survival depends on how well they work together in harmony, how they use their attributes, instincts, and skills, and how well they balance their individual vs. collective needs.

As settlers survive hardship and build up their settlements, they are rewarded with the pure joy of participating in this grand social experiment of creating a new branch of human society on another planetary surface.

Living and thriving on Mars is full of challenges and surprises for sure. There are no one single recipe for success that one can follow. But at the end, it will worth the sum of all our efforts as we are becoming a multi-planetary species continuing our stories of living among the stars.
